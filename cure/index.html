Is there a cure for COVID 19?

Not yet

There is currently no specific medication to prevent or treat COVID-19. However, those affected by the virus should seek and receive care to relieve the symptoms.

If you, or a loved one, have a serious illness, then it's best to seek medical attention immediately.. Most affected individuals see their symptoms subside.

Many who act early and consult their doctor are prevented from having a severe disease outcome.

Are there any home therapies that could help cure COVID?

NO

Note here that traditional and 'home remedies' may provide the affected person some comfort and even alleviate symptoms, BUT there is no evidence that current medication or home remedies could cure or prevent the disease.

(Consult a healthcare professional before starting any home treatment)

However, some self care tips and things found at home can provide comfort and help you recover faster.

  • If you show symptoms of COVID 19 or have tested positive, immediately isolate yourself from others at home
  • If you show symptoms of COVID 19 and have not yet tested yourself do so at the earliest

Self care tips:

  • Patients must be in communication with a treating physician and promptly report in case of any deterioration.
  • Continue to maintain all the preventive measures like hand washing, wearing a mask, maintaining a 6 feet distance from others, etc.
  • Wash your clothes separately and eat from different utensils.
  • Get a lot of rest. You're body needs to heal. Sleep can help your immune system function its best. Sleep atleast for 7-9 hours. Try to sleep & wake up at the same time everyday.
  • Relax & breathe slower. You can pair this with mindfulness/meditation.
  • Stay hydrated. Drink lots of water, fruit juices, liquids because your body loses water through sweat when you have fever. Stay away from soda or sugar filled drinks as they dehydrate you even more.
  • You may drink warm water/ warm liquids as it provides relief, however do not drink very hot liquids which may cause burns.
  • Maintain a healthy and beneficial diet
  • Continue the medications for other co-morbid illness after consulting the treating physician.
  • Patients to follow symptomatic management for fever, running nose and cough, as warranted. If you have fever or discomfort or body ache, you may take a paracetamol tablet ( Make sure you do not exceed 4 tablets in one day Do not take NSAIDs like Ibuprofen or any other medications without consulting your doctor )
  • Patients may perform warm water gargles with salt or take steam inhalation twice a day
  • For sore throat/cough, throat soothing lozenges provide relief. You may also consider anti-tussives(cough suppressant) for dry cough & expectorants(to remove the mucus) for cough with mucus.
  • Honey with tea or warm water also provides relief & works like a cough suppressant. Sucking on ginger or having ginger tea may also help.
  • Make sure you keep a check on your vitamin D levels ( if lesser than normal you may consider taking a vitamin D supplement after consulting a healthcare professional ) - for guidance or doubts feel free to contact us
  • Do not start any medications or vitamin supplements or natural remedies without consulting your doctor
  • Tab Ivermectin is safe to use and proven effective in preventing/reducing the severity of COVID symptoms. If you consider taking Ivermectin, do so after speaking to your doctor to know the correct protocol and dosage.
  • Maintain this symptom monitoring chart throughout the course of your illness

Home care tips for Mild COVID-19 Cases

The decision to administer Remdesivir or any other investigational therapy must be taken by a medical professional and administered only in a hospital setting. Do not attempt to procure or administer Remdesivir at home.

Systemic oral steroids not indicated in mild disease. If symptoms persist beyond 7 days (persistent fever, worsening cough etc.) or worsen, consult the treating doctor for further treatment.

How about using antibiotics to prevent or treat COVID-19?

Antibiotics do NOT work against viruses, they only work against bacterial infections. COVID-19 is caused by a virus. You should NOT use antibiotics as any form of prevention or treatment of COVID-19.

Antibodies will mostly only be prescribed by your doctor if you have a secondary bacterial infection along with the viral infection.

If cough worsens or you feel breathless;

EARLY AWAKE SELF-PRONING is advisable.

Prone positioning has been shown to improve oxygenation for decades.

PRONING is the process of turning a patient with precise, safe motions, from their back onto their abdomen (stomach), so the individual is lying face down.

Proning is a medically accepted position to improves breathing comfort and oxygenation. It is extremely beneficial in COVID-19 patients with compromised breathing comfort, especially during home isolation.

IMPORTANCE OF PRONING:

  • Prone positioning improves ventilation, keeps alveolar units open and breathing easy.
  • Missing out on hypoxia (compromised Oxygen circulation) may lead to worsening of complications.
  • Timing proning and maintaining good ventilation could save many lives.

Who can do it?

Consider Proning:

  • New/ worsening respiratory symptoms ( breathlessness, increasing or persistent cough, etc)
  • Hypoxia ( less Oxygen in blood )
  • If Patient is capable of Self-Proning and is able to communicate & co-operate

Avoid proning if any of the following conditions are present:

  • Pregnancy
  • Pressure >30mmHg, cerebral perfusion pressure <60mmHg or conditions with concerns for increasing ICP (intra-cranial hemorrhage)
  • Massive hemoptysis
  • Tracheal surgery or sternotomy during the previous 15 days
  • Serious facial trauma or facial surgery during the previous 15 days
  • Unstable spine, femur, or pelvic fractures
  • Deep venous thrombosis treated less than 48 hours
  • Cardiac pacemaker inserted in the last 48 hours/ major heart conditions
  • Unstable spine, femur, or pelvic fractures
  • Mean arterial pressure <65mmHg (OK to proceed if patient requiring vasoactives to keep MAP >65mmHg)
  • Single anterior chest tube with air leak
  • Chronic respiratory failure on home 02 or home BiPAP/CPAP
  • Patient on ECMO
  • Frequent ventricular arrhythmia
  • Provider or patient’s family making end of life decision/transition to CMO
  • Patient expected to die

If a patient is not able to prone on his own, please speak to a doctor. DO NOT TRY TO PRONE THEM WITHOUT GUIDANCE.

How to do it?

  1. For Self-Proning: You will need 4-5 Pillows. Regular alterations in lying position. Best is to not spend more than 30 minutes in each position.
  2. Positioning of pillows: One pillow below the neck; One or two pillows below the chest through upper thighs; Two pillows below the shins.
  3. Caution:
    • Avoid proning for an hour after meals
    • Maintain proning for only as much times as easily tolerable
    • One may prone for up to 16 hours a day, in multiple cycles, as felt comfortable
    • Pillows may be adjusted slightly to alter pressure areas and for comfort
    • Keep a track of any pressure sores or injuries, especially , around bony prominence
  4. Procedure: (at home)
    • Ensure patient has no contraindications as listed above or as deemed by provider
    • Remove any jewellery/objects on the body to prevent injury
    • Gather pillows and blankets to provide padding to pressure points
    • Follow the different positions as shown in the image

Stop immediately if any discomfort/ worsening.

When to discontinue home isolation?

Patients under home isolation can end isolation after at least 10 days(minimum) to 14 days from the onset of symptoms (or from date of sampling for asymptomatic cases) AND no fever for 3 days.

There is no need for testing after the home isolation period is over.
