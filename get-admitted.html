For standard symptoms of COVID-19, such as mild fever and cough, there is no need to go to the hospital or ER. Hospitals will send you home if the symptoms can be treated at home with self-care.

However, if you are feeling severely ill, it is advisable to go to the hospital.

Notify your healthcare provider

If you develop a fever, cough, and difficulty breathing, reach out and seek medical care immediately. Tell your healthcare provider of any recent travel, contact, or dealings with anyone who has the disease.

If you feel your condition is worsening or if your symptoms persist for over a week and you are concerned, please call a healthcare provider to evaluate you & to plan the further action.

(Feel free to reach out to us for guidance/ consultation and help regarding your situation.)

If you are experiencing the severe symptoms listed below, go to the nearest hospital or emergency room right away or call your state COVID helpline number.

  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Chest or upper abdominal pain or pressure
  • Sudden dizziness, weakness or fainting
  • Sudden change in vision / blurring vision
  • Confusion, sudden change in your mental status
  • Sudden or severe pain
  • Uncontrolled bleeding
  • Severe vomiting or diarrhea
  • Vomiting or coughing blood
  • Difficulty speaking/ loss of speech or movement
  • Low oxygen levels (<90% SpO2 reading)
  • Extreme sleepiness or inability to wake
  • Blue face, lips or hands/feet
  • Cold hands and feet
  • Reduced urine output
  • Fast heart rate/ weak pulse

Also the following people should consider going to the hospital after seeking advice from a healthcare professional

  • Immunocompromised patients with COVID symptoms
  • Patients with uncontrolled Blood sugar(diabetes) and Blood pressure(hypertension) with COVID symptoms
  • Cancer patients with COVID symptoms
  • Babies up to 1 year of age with temp >102-degree F

Home isolation or admission in Covid Care Centre is recommended for the following groups

  1. All asymptomatic patients
  2. Comorbid patients with no symptoms (prioritise to control the comorbid state)
  3. Mild symptoms
    • No comorbidities
    • Low fever (<100.4 F)
    • No signs of respiratory distress
    • Respiratory rate < 22/min
    • SpO2 >94%
    • Normal mental status
    • Systolic BP > 100 mmHg
  4. Even patients with comorbidities, of old age (>65 yrs) with COVID symptoms are being monitored and treated at home. Do speak to your healthcare provider before taking any decision as these patients have a high risk of becoming severe.
