Do you have COVID or is it just COMMON COLD?

Influenza or as we commonly call it, Flu and COVID-19 are both contagious respiratory illnesses, but are caused by different viruses. COVID-19 is caused by infection with a coronavirus, and flu is caused by infection with influenza viruses.

Compared to flu, COVID-19 causes more serious illnesses in people. COVID-19 also has a varied time before people show symptoms and people can be contagious for longer.

Symptoms of flu

Flu usually comes on suddenly. People who have flu often feel some or all of these symptoms:

  • fever or feeling feverish/chills
  • cough
  • sore throat
  • runny or stuffy nose
  • muscle or body aches
  • headaches
  • fatigue (tiredness)
  • some people may have vomiting and diarrhea, though this is more common in children as compared to adults.

1 or more days can pass between the person getting infected and for them to start showing symptoms of the illness

For how long can someone spread the Virus?

For both COVID-19 and flu, there can be spread of the virus for at least 1 day before experiencing any symptoms.

If a person has COVID-19, they could be contagious for a longer time than if they had flu, almost up to 10 - 14 days from the time of infection. People who are hospitalized with severe disease and people with weakened immune systems can be contagious for 20 days or longer. People with flu can be contagious from the 3rd to 7th day generally.

How the virus spreads

Both COVID-19 and flu can spread from person-to-person between people who are in close contact with one another (within about 6 feet). Both are spread mainly by large and small particles containing virus that are expelled when people with the illness (COVID-19 or flu) cough, sneeze, or talk. In indoor settings with poor ventilation, small particles might be spread further than 6 feet and cause infections. Infection is also possible if a person touches a surface that has the virus on it and then touches their mouth, nose or eyes.

COVID-19 is more contagious than flu viruses. Also, COVID-19 has been observed to have higher superspreading events than flu. This means the virus that causes COVID-19 can very easily spread to a lot of people.
